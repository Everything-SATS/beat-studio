❓ How to make a track

1. Build a beat

Pick a track on the left (Kick, Snare, Hi-hats are already there) and tap squares in the Pattern tab to turn steps on/off. Every 4th square is marked — that's one beat. Hit ▶ Play (or press Space) to hear it loop.

2. Add a bassline or melody

Select a synth track (or add one with the + button, choose "synth"). Its Pattern tab shows a piano roll instead of steps — click a cell to place a note (row = pitch, column = timing), click a placed note to delete it. Pick a preset first in the Instrument tab (Bass, Reese Bass, Acid Bass, Pad, Lead, etc.) — every knob under it is yours to tweak.

3. Shape the sound

The Mixer / FX tab has per-track volume, pan, EQ, distortion, chorus, delay, compressor and reverb send — plus master volume/compression/reverb at the bottom. Changes apply live while it's playing.

4. Build more patterns, then merge them into a song

One pattern is one loop (like a verse or a chorus). Click + New pattern to start another — your tracks keep their instrument/mixer settings, but each pattern has its own beat/notes per track. Switch the Pattern chips at the top of the editor to jump between them.

Once a pattern's ready, tap + Add current pattern in the song bar at the bottom — it appears as a chip in the arrangement strip. Repeat for every section (intro, verse, drop, outro...), in the order you want them to play. Switch the top-left toggle to Song mode and hit Play to hear the whole arrangement in sequence, not just one loop.

5. Record a vocal (optional)

🎤 Record Vocal captures your mic, then lets you snap it to a key/scale with an adjustable strength — 100% gives a hard, obvious "auto-tuned" character; lower settings are subtler. "Add as track" drops it in, triggered from the start of whichever pattern is playing.

6. Save and export

Everything autosaves to this browser as you work. 💾 Save / Load lets you keep named snapshots or export/import a full .json project file (for backup or moving devices). ⬇ Export WAV renders the current pattern (or the whole song, in Song mode) to a real audio file you can download and share anywhere.
